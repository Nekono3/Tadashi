Furthermore, later schedules allow parents more time to supervise morning routines, reducing the likelihood of teens skipping breakfast, which is critical for energy and focus.

Critics also argue that later start times might encourage teens to stay up even later, negating the benefits. Despite these concerns, evidence suggests that the advantages outweigh the drawbacks, as the positive effects on health and performance are well-documented.

As more schools adopt this change, researchers continue to monitor long-term effects, but the current evidence strongly supports later start times for teenagers.
